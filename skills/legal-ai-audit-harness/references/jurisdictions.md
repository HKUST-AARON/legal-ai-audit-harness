# Jurisdiction Profile Guide

Use these mappings when scoring `H` and `K`.

## Hong Kong

Profile: `hong_kong`

- `H`: Basic Law, ordinances, subsidiary legislation, Court of Final Appeal decisions, appellate decisions, first-instance decisions, persuasive common-law authorities.
- `K`: contrary appellate authority, distinguishing facts, statutory amendments, Basic Law constraints, persuasive overseas authority, later treatment.

## Mainland China

Profile: `mainland_china`

- `H`: Constitution, statutes, administrative regulations, judicial interpretations, guiding cases, typical cases, local regulations, ordinary judgments.
- `K`: competing statutory provisions, judicial interpretation limits, guiding/typical case distinctions, later amendments, local-rule conflicts.

## United States

Profile: `united_states`

- `H`: Constitution, federal/state statutes, regulations, binding appellate precedent, persuasive precedent, district decisions, secondary authority.
- `K`: contrary circuit/state authority, distinguishing precedent, overruled or superseded cases, statutory amendments, dissent/minority line.

## United Kingdom

Profile: `united_kingdom`

- `H`: statutes, delegated legislation, UK Supreme Court decisions, Court of Appeal decisions, High Court decisions, tribunal decisions.
- `K`: contrary appellate authority, distinguishing precedent, statutory interpretation alternatives, later treatment, persuasive Commonwealth authority.

## Germany

Profile: `germany`

- `H`: Basic Law, codes and statutes, ordinances, Federal Constitutional Court decisions, federal supreme court decisions, lower-court decisions, academic commentary.
- `K`: competing doctrinal interpretations, constitutional limits, systematic interpretation conflicts, later amendments, conflicting senate/court lines.

## Canada

Profile: `canada`

- `H`: Constitution, federal/provincial statutes, regulations, Supreme Court of Canada decisions, appellate decisions, trial decisions, persuasive authorities.
- `K`: contrary provincial appellate authority, Charter constraints, distinguishing precedent, statutory amendments, later treatment.
